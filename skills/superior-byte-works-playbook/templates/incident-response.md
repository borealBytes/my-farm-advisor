# Template: Incident Response Playbook

Use for `type: incident-response`. Activate when a threshold-crossing
event is detected or reported.

---

## Phase 1: Assess

**Trigger:** Incident reported or scouting threshold exceeded

| # | Action | Responsible | Tools | Duration | Success Criteria | Contingency |
|---|--------|-------------|-------|----------|-----------------|-------------|
| 1.1 | Confirm incident: re-scout affected area, photograph, GPS-mark | Operator | Phone/camera, GPS, scouting form | 1–2 hrs | Incident type and extent confirmed | If unable to ID: contact extension service |
| 1.2 | Determine affected acreage and spread potential | Operator | Field map, scouting data | 30 min | Acreage estimate ± 10% | Use conservative (larger) estimate if uncertain |
| 1.3 | Classify severity: Low / Medium / High / Critical | Operator | Threshold tables (see references) | 15 min | Severity class assigned | Default to one class higher if uncertain |

**Exit Criteria:** Incident confirmed, extent known, severity classified.

---

## Phase 2: Contain

**Trigger:** Severity ≥ Medium

| # | Action | Responsible | Tools | Duration | Success Criteria | Contingency |
|---|--------|-------------|-------|----------|-----------------|-------------|
| 2.1 | Isolate affected area (flag boundaries, adjust field traffic plan) | Operator | Flags, field map | 30 min | Boundaries marked, no unintended spread via equipment | Sanitize equipment between fields |
| 2.2 | Notify relevant parties (landlord, agronomist, crop insurance) | Operator | Phone, email | 1 hr | All required parties notified, documented | Log attempts if unreachable |
| 2.3 | Source appropriate input (pesticide, fungicide, amendment) | Operator | Supplier contact | Varies | Product on-hand or confirmed ETA | Identify alternative product if primary unavailable |

**Exit Criteria:** Spread arrested or mitigated, inputs sourced.

---

## Phase 3: Remediate

**Trigger:** Inputs available, weather window open

| # | Action | Responsible | Tools | Duration | Success Criteria | Contingency |
|---|--------|-------------|-------|----------|-----------------|-------------|
| 3.1 | Apply treatment per label rate and timing | Operator | Sprayer (calibrated), PPE | Varies | Full coverage; application conditions met (wind < 10 mph, no rain 6 hrs) | Reschedule if conditions not met |
| 3.2 | Record: product, rate, date, operator, field, equipment used | Operator | Application record | 15 min | Record complete and filed | — |

**Exit Criteria:** Treatment applied, records filed.

---

## Phase 4: Monitor

**Trigger:** 5–7 days post-treatment

| # | Action | Responsible | Tools | Duration | Success Criteria | Contingency |
|---|--------|-------------|-------|----------|-----------------|-------------|
| 4.1 | Re-scout: assess treatment efficacy against thresholds | Operator | Scouting form | 1 hr | Pressure below threshold | Trigger Phase 2 re-entry if threshold still exceeded |
| 4.2 | Adjust monitoring frequency based on pressure trajectory | Operator | Calendar | 15 min | Schedule set: weekly until clear | — |

**Exit Criteria:** Pressure below threshold on two consecutive scout dates.

---

## Phase 5: Close

| # | Action | Responsible | Tools | Duration | Success Criteria | Contingency |
|---|--------|-------------|-------|----------|-----------------|-------------|
| 5.1 | File incident close-out report: timeline, inputs used, cost, yield impact estimate | Operator | Records | 1 hr | Report complete | — |
| 5.2 | Update field history with incident data for future rotation planning | Operator | Farm records system | 30 min | Field record updated | — |

**Exit Criteria:** Incident closed, records updated.
